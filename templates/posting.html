Conversational AI Interruption Handling Study

Emory NLP Research Lab — Emory University

About This Study

We are recruiting participants to evaluate how AI-powered voice agents handle conversational interruptions across different personas and interaction styles.

In natural human conversation, interruptions are a critical part of turn-taking. However, most current voice AI systems handle interruptions poorly — either yielding immediately or ignoring the user entirely. This study investigates whether persona-aware interruption strategies can make voice AI feel more natural and human-like.

You will have voice conversations with various AI characters (e.g., a tavern keeper, a drill sergeant, a librarian) and evaluate how naturally they handle being interrupted.

Voice AI Conversational Agents Turn-Taking User Study HCI

What You Will Do

  • Have voice conversations with 24 AI personas through a web-based platform (microphone required)
  • Each conversation lasts approximately 2–4 minutes (15 turns)
  • You are encouraged to interrupt the AI during conversations — this is a key part of the study
  • After every 3 conversations, you will complete a brief comparative survey
  • Your progress is saved automatically — you can complete the study across multiple sessions
Estimated total time: 1.5 – 2 hours (can be split across multiple sessions)
Study period: 2 weeks from the date you sign up

Eligibility

  • Must be 18 years or older
  • Fluent in English
  • Access to a computer with a microphone and modern web browser (Chrome recommended)
  • Quiet environment for voice conversations

Compensation

$38 Amazon Gift Card upon completing all 24 sessions and surveys (~$25/hr effective rate, above the standard $15/hr student worker rate).

Compensation will be sent to the email address you provide after completion is verified.

Privacy & Data

  • Your audio is NOT recorded or saved — it is processed in real-time and discarded
  • Text transcripts of conversations are saved but fully anonymized — no personally identifiable information is linked to your responses
  • Survey responses are stored anonymously
  • Your personal information (name, student ID, email) is collected only for compensation purposes and kept separately from study data
